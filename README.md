1. Starting a business in Canada is confusing
Many people want to start a business but don’t know:
which structure to choose (sole proprietorship, partnership, corporation)
which registrations they need
what taxes apply
what legal documents are required
For example, they may need to register through agencies like the Canada Revenue Agency or provincial registries, but the information is scattered across many government websites. A platform that organizes everything in one place saves time and frustration.
2. Government information is complicated
Canadian government sites such as Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada and provincial business registries provide accurate information, but they are often:
hard to navigate
written in technical language
not step-by-step
Our website could simplify the process, like:
Choose business type
Register business name
Apply for a business number
Register for GST/HST if needed
Get permits or licenses
3. High demand from new entrepreneurs
Every year thousands of Canadians start businesses. Many people want:
side hustles
online businesses
consulting services
small local companies
But they don’t want to hire expensive lawyers or accountants at the beginning. A website that guides them cheaply or free could attract many users.
4. You can monetize it
The website could make money through:
Subscription plans for detailed guides
Document templates (contracts, partnership agreements)
AI tools that generate business registration steps
Affiliate partnerships with accountants or legal services
Business name search tools
Companies like LegalZoom and ZenBusiness already do similar things in the U.S., which shows the model works.
5. Opportunity for automation
You could build features like:
Business startup checklist generator
Province-specific requirements
Auto-generated legal documents
Fee calculator for registrations
For example, someone could select:
province: British Columbia
business type: sole proprietorship
And the site shows exact steps + fees + forms.
